1. The Inputs
The engine looks at two main datasets:

Your Profile: Annual Income, Age (derived from DOB), Marital Status, and Occupation.
Policy Details: Premium amount, Policy Type (Health, Life, Auto), and Coverage limits.
2. The Scoring Logic (Step-by-Step)
Every policy goes through this calculation:

A. Base Score

Start: Every policy starts with 50 points.
B. Affordability Check It calculates your monthly income and compares it to the policy's monthly premium:

Highly Affordable: If premium is < 5% of your monthly income -> +20 Points
Affordable: If premium is < 10% of your monthly income -> +10 Points
Expensive: If premium is > 10% of your monthly income -> -10 Points

If you are a 35-year-old Married Engineer earning $80k/year:

A Life Insurance policy costing $50/month would get:
Base: 50
Affordability ($50 is <5% of $6.6k/mo): +20
Life Stage (Married/Age>30): +20
Occupation (Engineer): +10
Total Score: 100/100 (Perfect Match)
